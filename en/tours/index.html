THE MAIN WALK

Historic Santa Marta
— Where Everything Began and Ended

Santa Marta is the oldest continuously inhabited city in South America. Founded in 1525, it was a slave port, a base for the conquistadors, and the city where the man who liberated six nations died — of a lung disease no doctor of the time could cure.

Plaza de Bolívar — the colonial heart since the 16th century, with the Cathedral Basilica facing it
Casa de la Aduana — where Simón Bolívar spent his last hours before dying in 1830
Republican architecture — bahareque houses, hidden courtyards, alleys the average tourist never sees
Local stories and legends — what the history books leave out, told by someone who grew up here
Hidden corners — secret plazas, forgotten façades, the Santa Marta that tourists never discover
Finish at El Sanatorio — welcome cocktail included at the end of the walk
Meeting point Plaza Bolívar, central statue

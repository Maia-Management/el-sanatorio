THE BUILDING · A HISTORY

Before El Sanatorio.

The building was part of Santa Marta’s historic medical complex. The Hospital del Tórax — an annex of the old Hospital San Juan de Dios — took in lung patients during the first wave of Caribbean tuberculosis, before streptomycin existed. The nurses worked twelve-hour shifts in white cotton uniforms and gauze masks that did not help much. They cared for one woman the house has always called La Bendita — half memory, half spirit, half guardian — and her name lives on in the bar and the kitchen today.

When the disease was finally controlled, the building closed. Sixty years passed. The green walls flaked, the crosses on the doors faded, the files were boxed up. One day we opened a box. The cards were still there. The names were still legible. And the patients — the ones you read about on this site — began to appear in the corridors.

“The building took care when it was its turn to take care. Almost a century later, it returns the favour to the people who do today what those nurses did for it then.”
